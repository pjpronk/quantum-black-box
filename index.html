The Quantum Black Box

The Quantum Black Box is a small square box, approximately 5cm in height and width, featuring walls with a thickness of 1nm. Within this confined space resides a solitary electron. The probability of this electron tunneling through the 1nm wall is approximately 1 in 1-e5. Given enough time and iterations the electron will be open the black box without the need to interact with it.

However, by pressing the white dot above, you can forcefully open the black box, liberating the electron. The consequences of unlocking the black box are unknown.
